# Design Brief — cash4homefl.com

## Best way to get design

The fastest, safest way to get a good design is:

1. **Pick one clear visual direction**
2. **Build a homepage and one city page prototype first**
3. **Lock the component system before scaling**
4. **Avoid multiple new tools or APIs**

For this site, the winning direction is:

- **premium minimal**
- **trust-first**
- **conversion-first**
- **local and human**

Think: a clean real-estate site that feels credible and modern, not a cheesy "we buy houses" template.

## Recommended visual posture

### Overall feel

- clean white or very light surface
- strong dark text
- one primary accent color
- simple card hierarchy
- large CTA buttons
- real photo sections
- very little decoration

### Suggested tone

- direct
- confident
- local
- reassuring
- not flashy
- not gimmicky

## Reference strategy

Use a **two-layer reference board**:

### Layer 1 — competitor research

Pull the best parts from:

- WeBuyUglyHouses
- FloridaCashHomeBuyers
- FloridaCashBuyers
- iBuyHouses
- SellMyHouseFast

Borrow the patterns, not the visuals:

- address-first hero
- trust badges
- simple 3-step explanation
- testimonial section
- FAQs
- city/service area links

### Layer 2 — premium product design cues

Use the polish of a strong product site rather than a dated real-estate theme.

Good references for visual posture:

- Vercel-like simplicity
- Stripe-like clarity
- Notion-like calm surfaces

## Design system recommendation

### Typography

- Use **Inter** or **Geist**
- Strong headline hierarchy
- No more than 2 font families
- Keep body copy highly readable

### Color strategy

- Background: off-white or pure white
- Text: charcoal / near-black
- Accent: one deep color for CTAs
- Trust color: muted green or blue for badges
- Avoid rainbow gradients and loud billboards

### Layout strategy

- hero with address form above the fold
- sticky phone / CTA on mobile
- trust row immediately below hero
- short sections with strong spacing
- testimonials with real faces and names
- FAQ accordion near the bottom

### Components to design first

- hero + address form
- trust bar
- CTA button styles
- testimonial card
- situation card
- FAQ accordion
- comparison table/card
- location links
- footer

## Best workflow for design production

### Step 1 — moodboard

Create a simple moodboard with:

- competitor screenshots
- 2 premium reference sites
- 1 local photo style reference
- 1 icon style reference

### Step 2 — prototype

Design just two screens first:

- homepage
- city page

If those two look good, the rest can be templated.

### Step 3 — lock tokens

Define:

- spacing scale
- type scale
- button styles
- card border radius
- shadow style
- accent color

### Step 4 — scale the system

Reuse the same layout and components across city, zip, and situation pages.

## Suggested design variants

If you want to explore before locking in:

1. **Conservative** — clean, white, trust-heavy
2. **Strong-fit** — premium minimal with bold CTA
3. **Divergent** — darker hero with higher contrast and more visual drama

For this project, the **Strong-fit** direction is the best default.

## Design deliverables to ask for

- homepage mockup
- city page mockup
- mobile mockup
- component library
- token sheet
- image placement guide
- CTA hierarchy

## What to avoid

- generic house clipart
- fake money imagery
- oversized gradients
- cheesy realtor stock photos
- too many icons
- too many colors
- over-designed testimonial cards
- cluttered hero sections
