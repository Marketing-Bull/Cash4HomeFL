# Content Brief — cash4homefl.com

## Best way to get content

Use a **structured content engine**:

1. store city / zip / county data in JSON
2. generate first-draft copy from templates
3. edit by hand for accuracy and tone
4. publish only after local proof is inserted

This keeps the site fast to build without needing a CMS or extra APIs.

## Content sources

Use content from:

- your own seller conversations
- Google reviews
- city and zip data
- neighborhood knowledge
- county-level pages
- prior deals and seller stories
- FAQs from real objections

## Page copy model

### Homepage

Needs:
- one strong value proposition
- address-first CTA
- 3-step process
- trust row
- situation grid
- comparison section
- FAQs
- final CTA

### City pages

Each city page should include:
- city-specific intro
- nearby neighborhoods or neighborhoods you serve
- local market note
- seller situations
- city-specific internal links
- city-specific FAQ answers
- CTA

### Zip pages

Each zip page should include:
- zip-specific title and intro
- parent city reference
- service-area context
- unique local angle or neighborhood mention
- CTA

### Situation pages

Each situation page should answer:
- what the problem is
- why cash buyers help
- how fast you can close
- what the seller should do next
- FAQs

### Blog posts

Use blog posts to support search intent and trust.

Good post ideas:
- selling a house as-is in Florida
- inherited house sale process
- foreclosure timeline and options
- cash buyer vs realtor
- how a fair cash offer is calculated

## Content workflow

### Step 1 — define the page brief

For each page, capture:

- primary keyword
- city or zip
- county
- page type
- seller pain point
- proof points
- CTA

### Step 2 — generate draft copy

Use the page brief to generate a first draft.

### Step 3 — human edit

Edit for:

- accuracy
- local specificity
- tone
- legal safety
- conversion quality

### Step 4 — insert proof

Add:

- testimonials
- Google review snippets
- local service area proof
- local phone number
- city/zip references

## Word-count guidance

- Homepage: 900–1400 words
- City page: 700–1100 words
- Zip page: 500–900 words
- Situation page: 900–1400 words
- Blog post: 1000–1600 words

## Content rules

- no fake stats
- no invented testimonials
- no filler paragraphs
- no duplicate city pages with only the city name changed
- no keyword stuffing
- no unnecessary fluff
- every page must answer a real seller question
